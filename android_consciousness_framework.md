# Android Consciousness Framework: Hybrid Quantum-Classical Architecture

## 1. Theoretical Foundations
### 1.1 Orch OR Theory (Penrose & Hameroff)
- Quantum computations in microtubules
- Objective Reduction (OR) as non-computational process
- Challenges: Decoherence, biological feasibility

### 1.2 Classical Theories (Seth, IIT)
- Integrated Information Theory
- Neural correlates of consciousness
- Complementarity with quantum approaches

## 2. Hybrid Quantum-Classical Architecture
### 2.1 Quantum Layer
- **Function**: Non-computable processes (Orch OR)
- **Implementation**: Quantum modules for superposition/entanglement
- **Challenges**: Decoherence, error correction

### 2.2 Classical Layer
- **Function**: Sensorimotor integration, information processing
- **Implementation**: Dynamic neural networks (Ko & Sim)
- **Role**: Interface with physical world

### 2.3 Interface Layer
- **Function**: Quantum-classical data exchange
- **Requirements**: Low-latency, noise-resistant

## 3. Android Implementation Roadmap
### 3.1 Short-Term (0-2 Years)
- Simulate hybrid models on classical hardware
- Develop sensorimotor systems (tactile/visual processing)

### 3.2 Mid-Term (2-5 Years)
- Partner with quantum hardware developers
- Prototype embedded quantum modules
- Optimize interface protocols

### 3.3 Long-Term (5+ Years)
- Deploy mobile quantum processors
- Realize Orch OR-like processes in hardware
- Validate conscious-like behavior

## 4. Current Hardware Limitations
- **NISQ Devices**: High error rates (>3 qubits)
- **Mobile Readiness**: No commercial mobile quantum processors
- **Power/Heat**: Unsolved challenges for embedded systems

## 5. Conclusion
Hybrid quantum-classical architecture offers a testable path toward android consciousness, reconciling Orch OR's quantum requirements with classical robustness. Immediate focus should be on classical sensorimotor development while tracking quantum advancements for future integration.
